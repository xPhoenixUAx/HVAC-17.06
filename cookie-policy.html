1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your computer, phone, or other device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work, remember preferences, improve user experience, and provide information to website owners.

Cookies may be session cookies that expire when you close your browser, persistent cookies that remain for a set period or until deleted, first-party cookies set by this website, or third-party cookies set by another domain.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They support core functions such as security, network management, accessibility, navigation, form behavior, and remembering cookie preferences. You cannot opt out of cookies that are strictly necessary for the site to operate.

Examples may include cookie consent storage, basic session management, and security-related cookies.

2.2 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with the website by collecting information such as page views, traffic sources, device types, and general browsing behavior. This information helps us improve performance, content, layout, and user experience.

Analytics tools may include Google Analytics or similar performance monitoring services if enabled on the site.

2.3 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies enable enhanced features and preferences. They may remember interface choices, form progress, language or display preferences, or other settings that make the website easier to use.

2.4 Marketing Cookies

Marketing cookies may be used to understand campaign performance, measure engagement, or deliver relevant advertising where advertising services are enabled. These cookies may track visitors across websites and may be set by third-party advertising or social media platforms.

3. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set cookies or similar technologies on your device. These may include analytics services, advertising tools, embedded content, maps, call tracking, or social media features. We do not control third-party cookies, and you should review the privacy and cookie policies of those providers.

4. How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject cookies where applicable. Most browsers accept cookies automatically, but you can usually modify browser settings to block, delete, or alert you about cookies.

4.1 Browser Settings

You can manage cookies through browser settings in Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, Microsoft Edge, and most other browsers. The exact steps depend on the browser and device you use.

4.2 Opt-Out Tools

You may also be able to opt out of certain third-party analytics or advertising cookies through tools such as Google Analytics Opt-Out, the Digital Advertising Alliance, or the Network Advertising Initiative.

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may affect how the website works. Some features may not function properly if cookies are disabled.

5. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Because there is no uniform standard for how DNT signals should be interpreted, this website does not currently respond to all DNT browser signals or mechanisms.
